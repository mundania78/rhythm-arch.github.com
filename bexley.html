Bexley Residence

This project proposed renovating a residence in a historic district and developing an addition to the rear that would both blend with the existing historic façade as well as differentiate itself as an addition.

The existing 4,000 s.f. residence was to be gutted completely leaving only the shell in-tact. This offered much needed flexibility to the plan layout to accommodate the needs of the client. The rear addition was to house the master suite on the upper level and additional living space off the kitchen on the main level. The existing façade was in disrepair and details needed to be created to restore the appearance of the building to its earlier aesthetics.

The finished product proved to be more elaborately structured than what was originally anticipated. The layout offered appropriate flexibility in the design to allow minor adjustments to be made once the building interior was undergoing demolition.
